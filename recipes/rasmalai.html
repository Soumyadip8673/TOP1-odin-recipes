Rasmalai

Description

Rasmalai is a rich and creamy Indian dessert made with soft, spongy cottage cheese balls soaked in a fragrant milk-based syrup. The cottage cheese balls, often called "malai," are typically made by kneading together crumbled paneer, milk powder, and cardamom powder.

The syrup, which is made from milk and sugar, is often flavored with cardamom and saffron, giving it a delicate and aromatic taste. The combination of the soft, spongy cottage cheese balls and the rich, creamy syrup makes rasmalai a luxurious and indulgent dessert.

  • Ingredients

    • 1/4 cup milk powder
    • 1 cup paneer (cottage cheese), crumbled
    • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
    • 2 cups milk
    • 1/4 cup sugar
    • 1/4 teaspoon saffron strands
    • 1/4 teaspoon rose water

  • Steps

    1. Make the paneer balls: Combine crumbled paneer, milk powder, and cardamom powder in a bowl. Knead into a dough and divide into small pieces. Shape into balls.
    2. Cook the paneer balls: In a saucepan, bring milk to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for a few minutes. Add the paneer balls and cook until they float to the surface.
    3. Make the syrup: In a separate saucepan, combine sugar and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the syrup thickens.
    4. Combine paneer and syrup: Add the cooked paneer balls to the syrup. Simmer for a few minutes, or until the paneer absorbs the syrup.
    5. Add saffron and rose water: Stir in saffron strands and rose water.
    6. Serve: Serve the rasmalai chilled, with a spoonful of the rich, creamy syrup.

Enjoy your delicious rasmalai!
