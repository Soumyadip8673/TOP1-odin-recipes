Fish Pakora

Description

Fish Pakora is a popular Indian appetizer that consists of deep-fried pieces of fish coated in a flavorful batter made of gram flour (besan), spices, and water. The batter gives the pakora a crispy exterior, while the fish inside remains tender and juicy.

The combination of the crispy batter and the flavorful fish makes fish pakora a delicious and satisfying starter. It's often served with dipping sauces like mint chutney or tamarind chutney to enhance the taste.

  • Ingredients

    • 1 pound fish fillets (cod, tilapia, or your preferred fish), cut into bite-sized pieces
    • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
    • 1/4 cup water
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon cumin powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • Oil for frying

  • Steps

    1. Prepare the batter: In a bowl, combine gram flour, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, garam masala, and salt. Gradually add water, mixing until you have a smooth, thick batter.
    2. Coat the fish:Dip each piece of fish into the batter, ensuring it is evenly coated.
    3. Fry the pakora: Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat. Carefully drop the coated fish pieces into the hot oil. Fry until golden brown and crispy, turning occasionally.
    4. Drain and serve:Remove the pakoras from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce, such as mint chutney or tamarind chutney.

Enjoy your delicious fish pakora!
