Chicken Curry

Description

Chicken Curry is a classic Indian main course dish that features tender pieces of chicken cooked in a flavorful tomato-based gravy. The gravy is typically seasoned with a variety of Indian spices, including cumin, coriander, turmeric, and chili powder, giving it a complex and aromatic taste.

The spiciness of the curry can vary depending on the amount of chili powder used, making it suitable for a wide range of palates. Chicken curry is often served with basmati rice or naan bread to soak up the delicious sauce.

  • Ingredients

    • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
    • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
    • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
    • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
    • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • 1 cup chicken broth
    • 1/4 cup heavy cream (optional)
    • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro

  • Steps

    1. Cook the onions: Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and cook until softened and golden brown.
    2. Add spices: Stir in the garlic, ginger, cumin, coriander, turmeric, red chili powder, and garam masala. Cook for 1 minute more.
    3. Add tomatoes: Add the diced tomatoes and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the tomatoes break down.
    4. Add chicken: Add the chicken pieces and cook until browned on all sides.
    5. Simmer: Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Cover and cook for 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through.
    6. Add cream (optional): If desired, stir in the heavy cream and simmer for a few minutes more.
    7. Serve: Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve hot with basmati rice or naan bread.

Enjoy your delicious chicken curry!
