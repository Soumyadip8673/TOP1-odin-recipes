Gulab Jamun

Description

Gulab Jamun is a popular Indian dessert that consists of deep-fried dough balls soaked in a sweet syrup flavored with rosewater and cardamom. The dough balls are typically made with milk powder, all-purpose flour, ghee, and a few other ingredients.

The deep-frying process gives the gulab jamuns a golden-brown exterior and a soft, chewy interior. The sweet syrup, flavored with rosewater and cardamom, adds a delightful aroma and taste. Gulab jamuns are often served warm and are a beloved dessert in many Indian households.

  • Ingredients

    • 1 cup milk powder
    • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)
    • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
    • 1/4 cup water
    • Oil for frying
    • 1 cup sugar
    • 1/4 cup water
    • 1/4 teaspoon rose water

  • Steps

    1. Make the dough: In a bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, ghee, baking powder, and cardamom powder. Add water and mix until a dough forms. Knead it until smooth. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
    2. Shape the jamuns: Divide the dough into small pieces and roll them into balls.
    3. Fry the jamuns: Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Fry the balls until golden brown and puffed up. Remove and drain on paper towels.
    4. Make the syrup: In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the syrup thickens.
    5. Soak the jamuns: Add the fried jamuns to the syrup and let them soak for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for better flavor.
    6. Serve: Serve the gulab jamuns warm, soaked in the sweet syrup and flavored with rose water.

Enjoy your delicious gulab jamun!
